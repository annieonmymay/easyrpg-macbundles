SUMMARY:
Fleshchild is a game about an infant, presumably extra-dimensional being named Vis growing up at the request/command of its parent, a giant red eyeball who lives in a sewer.

However, in order to grow, Vis will need to “borrow” a few parts from the lovely people living upstairs. The game begins with Vis’s first emergence in the cover of night. Now that they are free to wander outside the sewer, they must hunt down all the parts they need to make themselves a proper adult. Unfortunately, people won’t like lending Vis a hand if it costs an arm and a leg to do it!

GAMEPLAY:
This game is an exploration game, similar to and inspired by Yume Nikki. There are two main segments of the game: Reality and Dreaming

Reality - Vis will go to the city during the night to find parts. Gameplay involves wandering around buildings, collecting items to unlock doors and advance in the mission, reading notes and newspaper clippings to learn more about the story, and taking body parts from strangers, among a host of other things. At the end of a night, Vis will be called home to show their parent what they’ve accomplished, then go to sleep until the next night.

Dreaming - Vis’s dreams begin in the city, yet quickly spiral into surreal locations like a maze of brass piping, endless stark white hospital hallways, and a cabin in the woods. The player’s objective is to collect items called mutations; mutations are cosmetic forms Vis can take, like turning into a robot, and some come with abilities. However, Vis’s dreams end abruptly without warning. Thoroughly exploring one THEMED location (the entirety of the inside of a hospital, for example) before moving to the next will reward Vis with the best experience each dream has to offer.

WARNINGS:
This game features copious amounts of visual violence, gore, blood, body horror, and a host of other things. Be wary of playing if you are sensitive to any of the above.

CONTROLS:

Arrow Keys - Movement
Space - Interact/Confirm/Examine
Shift - Run in the Real World/Use ability in Dream World
1 - Puddle/blob form in the Real World
ESC - Menu

Save: Use the "S" shaped flowers. You'll know you found a save room when the BGM changes. You'll encounter your first save flower in the first area you explore.

Body Part Deposits: Use these to remove all human body parts, or put them all back on. Some puzzles require puzzle/blob form or human form to complete. They look like a big fleshy tube. You'll encounter your first part deposit in the first area you explore.

/////HOW TO INSTALL PATCHES: DETAILED

FIRST: ALWAYS MAKE A BACKUP OF THE GAME (Copy Fleshchild's folder, paste in your game folder, name it Fleshchild Backup or something)

1. Download the correct patch (the correct patch link will always be updated on FC's blog page: http://bleetdev.tumblr.com/fleshchild )
2. Move Patch to Fleshchild's MAIN folder
3. Extract ALL map.lmu files to the MAIN folder
4. Make sure to choose either MOVE & REPLACE or COPY & REPLACE for all choices (Windows)

If patches are uploaded, it is VERY IMPORTANT that you install them!
